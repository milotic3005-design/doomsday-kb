# IV Drug Preparation and Stability Reference

## USP <797> Beyond-Use Date (BUD) Reference

USP <797> sets standards for compounded sterile preparations (CSPs). BUD depends on sterility risk level and storage conditions.

### Risk Category Definitions

**Category 1 (Low Risk — formerly Low-Risk)**:
- Compounding involves limited manipulations (e.g., single transfer from ampule/vial).
- ISO 5 (laminar airflow hood) environment required.
- BUD: **12 hours at room temperature, 24 hours refrigerated** (without sterility testing).

**Category 2 (Medium Risk — formerly Medium-Risk)**:
- Multiple manipulations, pooling, complex compounding.
- ISO 5 environment with ISO 7 buffer room required.
- BUD: **1 hour at room temperature, 12 hours refrigerated** (without sterility testing).

**RT = Room Temperature (20-25°C / 68-77°F)**
**SWFI = Sterile Water for Injection**
**NS = Normal Saline (0.9% NaCl)**
**D5W = Dextrose 5% in Water**

## Reconstitution Procedures

### General Steps
1. Verify drug name, strength, expiration date, and lot number.
2. Calculate the required dose and diluent volume.
3. Swab all vial stoppers and ampule necks with 70% isopropyl alcohol. Allow to dry.
4. Using aseptic technique, inject diluent into powder vial.
5. Swirl gently (do not shake — causes foaming and may denature proteins).
6. Ensure powder is completely dissolved — solution should be clear.
7. Withdraw the required dose.
8. Further dilute in appropriate IV fluid (NS, D5W, or LR as specified).
9. Label with: drug, dose, patient, date/time, BUD, preparer initials.

### IV Push vs. IV Piggyback

**IV Push (IVP)**:
- Small volume (typically 1-10mL) injected directly into IV line.
- Must be given at specified rate (e.g., "over 3-5 minutes").
- Used when rapid onset needed or small volumes.

**IV Piggyback (IVPB)**:
- Drug diluted in 50-250mL bag of compatible fluid.
- Infused over 15-60 minutes typically via secondary line.
- Most common method for intermittent IV medications.

**Continuous Infusion**:
- Drug in large volume bag (250-1000mL).
- Infused continuously over hours to days.
- Examples: vasopressors, insulin, heparin, electrolyte replacements.

## Dosage Calculations

### Basic Formulas

**Desired dose from available concentration**:
Volume (mL) = (Desired dose in mg) ÷ (Concentration in mg/mL)

Example: Need vancomycin 750mg from a 50mg/mL solution:
750 ÷ 50 = 15 mL

**Infusion rate**:
Rate (mL/hr) = (Volume in mL) ÷ (Time in hours)

Example: 250mL bag over 1 hour = 250 mL/hr

**Drip rate (without pump)**:
Drops/min = (Volume × Drop factor) ÷ (Time in minutes)

Standard IV tubing: 15 drops/mL (macrodrip)
Microdrip tubing: 60 drops/mL

Example: 100mL over 30 min with macrodrip:
(100 × 15) ÷ 30 = 50 drops/min

### Weight-Based Dosing
Dose = Patient weight (kg) × mg/kg dose

Example: Vancomycin 15mg/kg for 70kg patient:
15 × 70 = 1050mg (round to 1000mg)

### Concentration Calculations

**Percentage solutions**:
- 1% = 10 mg/mL (or 10 g/L)
- 0.9% NaCl = 9 mg/mL = 9 g/L
- D5W = 50 mg/mL dextrose = 50 g/L

**mcg/kg/min (common for vasopressors)**:
Rate (mL/hr) = (Dose in mcg/kg/min × Weight in kg × 60) ÷ (Concentration in mcg/mL)

## Drug Compatibility

### Y-Site Compatibility (Can be given simultaneously through same IV line)

**Generally Compatible**:
- NS + most antibiotics (run as IVPB)
- Potassium chloride + most drugs (in appropriate concentration)

**NEVER Mix (Precipitate or Inactivation)**:
- Phenytoin + dextrose solutions (crystallizes — use NS only)
- Ampicillin + aminoglycosides (inactivation)
- Ceftriaxone + calcium-containing solutions (fatal precipitation)
- Sodium bicarbonate + calcium (precipitation)
- Diazepam + most IV drugs (incompatible with most solutions)

### Rule of Thumb
When unsure about compatibility: **flush the line with 10-20mL NS between medications.** This is always the safest approach.

## Controlled Substance Handling

### High-Alert Medications
These require independent double-checks before administration:
- Insulin (all formulations)
- Opioids (morphine, fentanyl, hydromorphone)
- Anticoagulants (heparin, warfarin)
- Concentrated electrolytes (KCl >20mEq, MgSO4 >4g)
- Neuromuscular blocking agents
- Chemotherapy agents

### Opioid Equianalgesic Doses (Approximate)
| Drug | IV/IM Dose | Oral Dose |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Morphine | 10mg | 30mg |
| Hydromorphone | 1.5mg | 7.5mg |
| Fentanyl | 100mcg | N/A (transdermal) |
| Oxycodone | N/A | 20mg |
| Hydrocodone | N/A | 30mg |

To convert between opioids:
1. Calculate current total daily dose.
2. Convert to morphine equivalents using table above.
3. Convert from morphine equivalents to new drug.
4. Reduce by 25-50% for cross-tolerance (the body is not equally tolerant to a new opioid).
