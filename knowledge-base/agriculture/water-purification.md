# Water Purification and Food Preservation

## Water Purification Methods

Water is the most critical survival resource. An adult needs 2-4 liters per day minimum.

### Boiling
- **The most reliable method.** Kills bacteria, viruses, parasites, and cysts.
- Bring water to a rolling boil for **at least 1 minute** (3 minutes above 2000m / 6500ft elevation).
- Let cool naturally. Pour between containers to improve taste (adds oxygen).
- Does NOT remove chemical contaminants or heavy metals.

### Chemical Treatment

**Household Bleach (Sodium Hypochlorite 5-8%)**
- Use UNSCENTED regular bleach only.
- Dose: 2 drops per liter for clear water, 4 drops per liter for cloudy water.
- Mix well and wait 30 minutes before drinking.
- Water should have a slight chlorine smell — if not, repeat the dose and wait 15 more minutes.
- Bleach degrades over time — after 1 year, potency drops significantly.

**Iodine**
- Iodine tablets or 2% tincture of iodine.
- Dose: 5 drops per liter (clear water) or 10 drops (cloudy water). Wait 30 minutes.
- NOT recommended for pregnant women, thyroid conditions, or iodine allergy.
- Does not kill Cryptosporidium cysts effectively.

### Filtration
- **Ceramic filters**: Remove bacteria and parasites. Pore size 0.2 microns typical. Must be paired with chemical treatment for viruses.
- **Activated carbon**: Removes chemicals, taste, odor, some pesticides. Does NOT remove bacteria.
- **Sand filter (DIY)**:
  1. Layer in a container (top to bottom): fine sand → coarse sand → gravel → rocks.
  2. Pour water through slowly.
  3. This is a pre-filter — MUST be followed by boiling or chemical treatment.

### Solar Disinfection (SODIS)
- Fill clear PET plastic bottles (1-2 liters) with water.
- Place in direct sunlight for 6+ hours (2 days if cloudy).
- UV radiation kills most pathogens.
- Works best with clear water — pre-filter if cloudy.
- Does NOT work through glass (UV-blocking).

## Rainwater Collection

1. Set up clean collection surfaces (tarps, roofing material, clean plastic sheeting).
2. Divert first 5 minutes of rain ("first flush") — this washes roof contaminants.
3. Store in dark, covered containers to prevent algae growth.
4. Still treat before drinking (bird droppings, airborne contaminants).

## Food Preservation

### Drying / Dehydration
- Remove moisture to prevent bacterial growth. Target moisture content: below 10%.
- **Sun drying**: Slice food thin (3-6mm). Place on clean racks in direct sunlight. Protect from insects with mesh. Takes 2-4 days.
- **Smoke drying**: Build a smoke rack over a low fire. Hardwoods (hickory, oak) work best. Avoid resinous woods (pine, spruce). 12-24 hours.
- **Test for dryness**: Meat should crack when bent. Fruits should be leathery with no moisture when squeezed.

### Salt Curing
- Pack meat in salt (ratio: 1 part salt to 4 parts meat by weight).
- Or make a brine: 1 cup salt per 4 cups water.
- Refrigeration extends life, but salt curing alone preserves at room temperature for weeks to months.

### Fermentation
- **Sauerkraut**: Shred cabbage, add 2% salt by weight, pack tightly in a jar, weigh down to keep submerged, cover loosely. Ferments in 1-4 weeks at room temperature.
- **Fermented vegetables**: Same process with any vegetables (carrots, radishes, peppers).
- **Vinegar preservation (pickling)**: Submerge vegetables in vinegar (at least 5% acidity).

### Root Cellar Storage
- Underground storage maintains 32-40°F (0-4°C) year-round.
- Store root vegetables (potatoes, carrots, beets, turnips) in damp sand.
- Apples can last 4-6 months. Keep separate from other produce (ethylene gas).
- Maintain 85-95% humidity to prevent drying.

## Basic Crop Growing

### Soil Assessment
- **pH testing**: Most vegetables prefer pH 6.0-7.0. Add lime to raise pH, sulfur to lower.
- **Drainage test**: Dig a 12-inch hole, fill with water. If it drains within 4 hours, drainage is adequate.
- **Texture**: Grab a handful of moist soil and squeeze. Good soil forms a ball that crumbles when poked.

### High-Priority Survival Crops
1. **Potatoes**: High calorie density. Plant eyes/seed potatoes 4 inches deep, 12 inches apart. 70-120 days to harvest.
2. **Beans (dried)**: High protein. Direct sow after last frost. Dry on vine, shell, and store.
3. **Squash (winter)**: Long storage life. Full sun, rich soil. Cure in sun for 10 days after harvest.
4. **Corn**: Staple grain. Needs full sun and space. Can be dried and ground into flour.
5. **Greens (kale, collards)**: Cold hardy, nutrient dense. Cut-and-come-again harvest.

### Seed Saving
- Let the strongest plants produce seed (do not eat them).
- Allow seeds to fully mature and dry on the plant.
- Store seeds in cool, dark, dry conditions. Paper envelopes in a sealed jar with silica gel.
- Most vegetable seeds remain viable 2-5 years if stored properly.
- **Cross-pollination warning**: Keep different varieties of the same species separated by 100+ feet, or bag flowers.
