## QUICK REFERENCE CHEAT SHEET

### Critical Numbers to Memorize
- **Rule of Threes:** 3 min air, 3 hrs severe weather, 3 days water, 3 weeks food
- **Tourniquet:** Apply 2–3" above wound; time-to-limb-loss without: ~6 hours
- **Shock Index:** HR ÷ SBP > 1.0 = shock present
- **Burn fluid:** 4 mL × kg bodyweight × % BSA burned in 24 hrs (Parkland)
- **ORS:** 1L water + 2.6g NaCl + 13.5g glucose + 1.5g KCl + 2.9g trisodium citrate
- **Boiling altitude:** 1 min at sea level; 3 min above 6,500 ft
- **Bleach (6.5%):** 2 drops/liter clear water; 4 drops/liter cloudy water; wait 30 min
- **Declination:** East = subtract from map bearing; West = add (East is Least, West is Best)
- **Pace count:** ~2,000 paces per mile on flat terrain (calibrate yours)
- **NVIS height:** 40–55 ft for regional HF coverage
- **EpiPen:** Adult 0.3mg IM outer thigh; may repeat once at 5–15 min if no response
- **Acetazolamide (altitude):** 125mg every 12 hrs starting 24 hrs before ascent
- **Concrete (general purpose):** 1 part cement : 2 parts sand : 3 parts gravel; W/C 0.45–0.55

### Emergency Frequencies
- **NOAA Weather:** 162.400–162.550 MHz (7 channels, check local)
- **Marine Distress:** Channel 16 (156.800 MHz)
- **Amateur Calling (2m):** 146.520 MHz simplex
- **Amateur Calling (HF/40m):** 7.200–7.300 MHz SSB; 7.038 MHz CW
- **APRS (North America):** 144.390 MHz
- **Air-to-Ground (Civil):** 121.500 MHz (international distress)
- **CB Channel 9:** 27.065 MHz (emergency)

### Universal Edibility Test (abbreviated)
1. Fast 8 hours → 2. Skin contact test (inner wrist, 15 min) → 3. Lip test → 4. Tongue tip → 5. Chew small piece, don't swallow, 15 min → 6. Swallow small amount, wait 8 hrs → 7. If no reaction: eat larger amount, wait 8 hrs → PASS
