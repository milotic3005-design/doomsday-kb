# Essential Medications Reference

## Pain Management

### Acetaminophen (Tylenol/Paracetamol)
- **Use**: Mild-moderate pain, fever reduction.
- **Adult dose**: 500-1000mg every 4-6 hours. MAX 3000mg/day (2000mg if liver concerns).
- **Child dose**: 10-15 mg/kg every 4-6 hours.
- **Warnings**: Liver toxicity at high doses. No alcohol. Overdose antidote: N-acetylcysteine (NAC).

### Ibuprofen (Advil/Motrin)
- **Use**: Pain, inflammation, fever.
- **Adult dose**: 200-400mg every 4-6 hours. MAX 1200mg/day (OTC), 2400mg/day (prescription).
- **Child dose**: 5-10 mg/kg every 6-8 hours.
- **Warnings**: GI bleeding risk. Not with kidney disease. Take with food. Avoid in pregnancy.

### Naproxen (Aleve)
- **Use**: Pain, inflammation (longer duration than ibuprofen).
- **Adult dose**: 220-500mg every 8-12 hours. MAX 1250mg/day.
- **Warnings**: Same as ibuprofen. Do not combine NSAIDs.

## Antibiotics

### Amoxicillin
- **Use**: Respiratory infections, ear infections, urinary tract infections, dental infections.
- **Adult dose**: 500mg every 8 hours or 875mg every 12 hours.
- **Child dose**: 25-50 mg/kg/day divided every 8 hours.
- **Duration**: 7-10 days typically.
- **Warnings**: Penicillin allergy = do not use. Diarrhea common.

### Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin)
- **Use**: Bite wounds, skin/soft tissue infections, UTIs, sinus infections.
- **Adult dose**: 875/125mg every 12 hours.
- **Duration**: 7-10 days.
- **Warnings**: Higher diarrhea rate. Take with food.

### Azithromycin (Z-Pack)
- **Use**: Respiratory infections, STIs, traveler's diarrhea, skin infections.
- **Adult dose**: 500mg day 1, then 250mg days 2-5 (standard Z-pack).
- **Warnings**: QT prolongation risk. Avoid with heart rhythm medications.

### Ciprofloxacin
- **Use**: UTIs, GI infections, anthrax exposure, bone/joint infections.
- **Adult dose**: 500mg every 12 hours.
- **Duration**: 7-14 days depending on infection.
- **Warnings**: Tendon rupture risk. Avoid in children and pregnancy. Photosensitivity.

### Doxycycline
- **Use**: Tick-borne diseases (Lyme, Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever), MRSA, respiratory infections, malaria prevention.
- **Adult dose**: 100mg every 12 hours.
- **Duration**: 7-21 days depending on condition.
- **Warnings**: Photosensitivity. Do not take with dairy/antacids. Can cause esophageal irritation — take upright with full glass of water.

### Metronidazole (Flagyl)
- **Use**: Anaerobic infections, dental infections, GI parasites (Giardia), C. diff.
- **Adult dose**: 500mg every 8 hours.
- **Warnings**: Absolutely NO alcohol (severe reaction). Metallic taste.

### Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (Bactrim)
- **Use**: UTIs, MRSA skin infections, traveler's diarrhea, Pneumocystis pneumonia.
- **Adult dose**: 1 DS tablet (160/800mg) every 12 hours.
- **Warnings**: Sulfa allergy. Can cause Stevens-Johnson syndrome (rare but serious). Sun sensitivity.

## Anti-Diarrheal / GI

### Loperamide (Imodium)
- **Use**: Acute non-bloody diarrhea.
- **Adult dose**: 4mg initially, then 2mg after each loose stool. MAX 16mg/day.
- **Do NOT use with**: Bloody diarrhea, fever, suspected bacterial dysentery.

### Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)
- **Critical for**: Dehydration from diarrhea, vomiting, heat illness.
- **Recipe**: 1 liter clean water + 6 teaspoons sugar + 1/2 teaspoon salt.
- **Administer**: Small frequent sips. 200-400mL after each loose stool (adult).
- **Signs of severe dehydration**: Sunken eyes, skin tenting, absent tears, lethargy, rapid weak pulse.

## Antihistamines

### Diphenhydramine (Benadryl)
- **Use**: Allergic reactions, anaphylaxis (adjunct), insomnia, itching.
- **Adult dose**: 25-50mg every 6-8 hours.
- **Warnings**: Heavy sedation. Do not operate machinery.

### Epinephrine (EpiPen)
- **Use**: ANAPHYLAXIS — life-threatening allergic reaction.
- **Adult dose**: 0.3mg IM (intramuscular) into outer thigh. Can repeat every 5-15 minutes.
- **Child dose**: 0.15mg IM.
- **Signs of anaphylaxis**: Throat swelling, difficulty breathing, widespread hives, drop in blood pressure, rapid pulse.
- **This is a LIFE-THREATENING EMERGENCY. Administer immediately.**

## Topical Medications

### Bacitracin / Triple Antibiotic Ointment
- **Use**: Prevention of infection in minor wounds.
- **Apply**: Thin layer to clean wound 2-3 times daily.

### Hydrocortisone Cream (1%)
- **Use**: Itching, rashes, insect bites, contact dermatitis.
- **Apply**: Thin layer 2-3 times daily. Do NOT use on infected skin or open wounds.

### Povidone-Iodine (Betadine)
- **Use**: Wound antiseptic, surgical prep.
- **Use diluted** (10:1 with clean water) for wound irrigation. Full strength for skin prep.
- **Warnings**: Iodine allergy. Do not use deep in wounds (cytotoxic to healing tissue at full strength).
